Nuclear Fission

Nuclear Fission is a process in which a heavy atom, that is typically uranium in current nuclear power plants, is split into smaller atoms which release a large amount of energy. This process is dangerous if people don't wear the right equipment as uranium is radioactive and when it's split, the products the reaction has are also radioactive.

Nuclear Fission isn't Infinite

Unlike Nuclear Fusion, Nuclear Fission is not green energy and not infinite as the element used in nuclear power plants is uranium and it isn't renwable as it would take thousands to millions of years for uranium to resupply. That doesn't mean that we shouldn't use nuclear fission or that it's unsafe, however.

Safety of Nuclear Fission

There is a misconception that Nuclear Fission is unsafe and something that is commonly referenced is the Chernobyl diaster in Ukraine in 1986, with Chernobyl still uninhabitable. Though since then, the advancements and knowledge in nuclear fission have increased with more safety measures with rigorous protocols with the automatic shutdown of nuclear reactors if something were to go wrong as an example, all to ensure safety. While you may still feel uneasy about nuclear power, know that now the probability of a nuclear diaster in general is 10-9 to 10-10 per year which practically means that a nuclear diaster is not plausible.
