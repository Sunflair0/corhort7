The Sun

There's really only one example of Nuclear Fusion in nature and that would be the stars in our universe. The sun, for example, like all other stars use nuclear fusion to power themselves. The nuclear fusion reaction happens in the core of the sun where the pressure is 26.5 million gigapascals and the temperature is 27 million degrees fahrenheit! When the fusing is done and the energy is made, that same energy won't make its way to the surface for 200,000 years! These nuclear fusion reactions will continue to occur until the sun or any star, runs out of hydrogen atoms, but that won't happen to our star for about 5 billion years, for the sun will then become a tiny white dwarf star.
