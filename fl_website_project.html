Nuclear Fusion

What is Nuclear Fusion?

Nuclear fusion is basically hydrogen atoms fusing together to produce a heavier atom and releasing massive amounts of energy as a byproduct. For fusion reactions to occur, they must first have the hydrogen isoptopes known as deuterium and tritium. Next, the isotopes must be in an environment where the pressure is extremely high and the isotopes are heated up exponentially to a plasma state. Finally, when those conditions are met, the isotopes collide in at very high speeds and fuse together, which would create a heavier atom, which would typically be helium, and create a bunch of energy. Fusion reactions are where stars like the sun get their power.

What The Difference Between Nuclear Fusion and Fission is

The difference between nuclear fusion and nuclear fission is that nuclear fusion is combining two light atoms which are typically hydrogen, to form a heavier atom, which releases energy. Nuclear fission on the other hand, is the splitting of a heavy atom, typically uranium into smaller fragments which release energy. Fission reactions are unsafer than fusion reactions as the process of splitting an atom is more dangerous than fusing one. Fission reactions also produce long-lived radioactive waste while fusion produces minimal long-lived radioactive waste, though fission waste can be disposed of safely just like fusion waste due technological advancements. While they both release energy, nuclear fission energy is not infinite unlike nuclear fusion energy, but both are clean means of energy and i go more into depth here.

Effects of Nuclear Fusion

There are many effects of Nuclear Fusion, both positive and negative, but I'll start out with the positive and those Positive Effects include:

  • Abundant Fuel Supply: Fusion relies on the atom hydrogen and its isotopes, which are quite abundant due to it being the simplest element in the universe with only 1 proton and electron.
  • Clean Energy Production: Fusion produces energy without emitting greenhouse gases or long-lived radioactive waste, making fusion a clean energy source.
  • Safety: Fusion reactions are safer than fission reactions as said ealier, with no risk of runaway chain reaction or a large-scale nuclear meltdown.
  • Low Radioactive Waste: Fusion reactions generate minimal long-lived radioactive waste compared to fission which simplifies its disposal and reduces its environmental impact
Now that we've gone over the postive effects of nuclear fusion let's go over the Negative Effects which include:
  • Technical Challeges: Achieving and maintaining the extreme conditions needed for sustained fusion reactions is a challenge as we've only been able to recreate fusion reactions for a short amount of time and need more advanced materials.
  • High Cost: Building and maintaining the specilized infrastructure is very expensive as is the cost to researching nuclear fusion.
  • Energy Input VS. Output: Most fusion experiments lead to more energy being inputted than being output, there were however, two experiments which led to a net positive result though more research is needed to learn how to get that result more often and maintained.
  • Timeline To Make Marketable: The timeline for achieving a commercially viable fusion energy is unknown due to the many experiments still needed to learn how to produce a fusion reaction more frequently and maintain it.
Overall, the effects of Nuclear Fusion are positve and has no real negatives, just the questions of how and when we are going to achieve nuclear fusion energy.
